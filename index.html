Original IP · Research-Cited Framework

MTSS Intervention Flowchart, 2026–27

Tiered support framework with parallel academic and behavior/SEL lanes

Designed an integrated, research-cited Multi-Tiered System of Supports framework that runs academic and behavior/SEL interventions in parallel — not as separate fiefdoms. Built around evidence-based thresholds and the principle that data below threshold is a signal to review Tier 1 fidelity, not a referral trigger.

  • 80–85% Tier 1 benchmark — if fewer than 80% of students meet goals, evaluate instruction and curriculum, not students.
  • ICEL root-cause framework (Instruction, Curriculum, Environment, Learner) drives diagnosis before prescription.
  • Cited research base — 30+ peer-reviewed sources, including Fuchs & Fuchs (2006), IRIS Center (2022), Zhang et al. (2023), Thomas/Lembke/Gandhi (2023), NCII/AIR (2024).

Case Study 01 · Performance Turnaround

Schoolwide Performance Turnaround

Inaugural Assistant Principal · Title I Middle School (~1,000 students)

Challenge

A newly opened middle school faced inconsistent instructional practices, no aligned operating systems, and low baseline performance. Teams were working hard — but not in coordinated directions — and student outcomes reflected that.

Action
  • Built and led a data-driven Professional Learning Community (PLC) operating system with consistent rhythms for planning, review, and adjustment.
  • Implemented Plan-Do-Study-Act (PDSA) cycles across grade-level and content teams to drive continuous improvement.
  • Delivered targeted professional development on MTSS, Universal Design for Learning (UDL), and high-impact instructional clarity.
  • Established schoolwide instructional expectations and the monitoring infrastructure to maintain them.
Results
  • 10–15% increase in overall performance outcomes in a single year.
  • 65%+ of students improved scores in both ELA and Mathematics.
  • +6.6 point gain on California Science Test (CAST).
  • Contributed directly to California Distinguished School recognition.
Corporate Translation

Enterprise-wide performance improvement initiative · cross-functional team alignment · KPI-driven execution under measurable goals.

Case Study 02 · System Architecture

MTSS System Design for Intervention

Tiered support architecture and decision framework

Challenge

Existing intervention processes were symptom-focused rather than system-focused, leading to over-referral, inconsistent decisions, and inefficient use of staff time and student time.

Action
  • Designed and implemented a tiered support decision framework (MTSS) that routed students by objective criteria, not anecdote.
  • Introduced an "80% rule" trigger model — if Tier 1 wasn't reaching 80% of students, the issue belonged to instruction, not the student.
  • Trained staff in root-cause analysis using the ICEL framework (Instruction, Curriculum, Environment, Learner) to diagnose before prescribing.
  • Built the framework now published as the MTSS Intervention Flowchart, 2026–27 (see Section 01).
Results
  • Reduced unnecessary intervention referrals.
  • Improved Tier 1 instructional effectiveness across grade levels.
  • Increased staff clarity and consistency in support decisions.
Corporate Translation

Process optimization · decision-making framework design · operational efficiency · root-cause methodology applied at scale.

Section 03 · How I Work

The operating commitments behind every framework, project, and team I've led.

Systems for Students First

When outcomes fall short, the instinct is to look at students first. I start somewhere different. I examine the structures and systems surrounding students, because that is where the answers most often live. Every decision begins and ends with one question: what does this do for student learning and wellbeing?

Design for All

Every educator enters this profession believing every child can learn. The challenge is building systems that make that belief a daily reality, not an aspiration. I help organizations design pathways where every learner has genuine access to the standards every facilitator commits to teaching — with the supports that make access real, not theoretical. Not about doing more. About designing better.

Burden Resolution

People are not burned out because they stopped caring. They are burned out because the systems around them were not designed to work coherently. I work to close that gap. When curriculum, instruction, and support are aligned around a shared purpose, the system begins doing the work it was supposed to do — and the burden begins to resolve. Not because the work gets easier, but because it finally makes sense.

Moral Clarity

Most practitioners already know what their people need. The harder question is what to do when the system, the politics, or the pressure make acting on that knowledge feel impossible. I believe knowing what people need — and having the professional courage to act on it, even when uncomfortable — is the defining responsibility of leadership. Not as a judgment. As a commitment we make together.
